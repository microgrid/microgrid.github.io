Discover the Country

Enter in the most magical and mysterious of all the Himalayan nations.


Bhutan refers as “land of the thunder dragon” remains a mysterious and preserved Himalayan kingdom with a development philosophy based on Gross National Happiness.This country emerging from its isolation has become unique by its growing development and strategy of sustainable tourism while conserving its traditional culture, religious festivals, and untouched environment. Most of the Bhutanese people live in rural villages through farms activities however Bhutan has one of the fastest growing economy in the world resulting from high potential of hydropower and an agriculture allowing livelihood for half of the population.

You can visit the official website of Bhutan bhutan.gov

Energy Sector in Bhutan

Bhutan is rated “role model” as it has communicated its goal of remaining carbon neutral.


In the eleventh year plan of Bhutan, the main target is to use the existing potential in hydropower and renewable energies to provide energy security and a sustainable development. The solar power capacity multiplied by 7 and the use of biogas are intended for 2018.

Project

Developing design tools for dimensioning of a PV-microgrid in Bhutan


Do you want to use your knowledge to do a meaningful project and master thesis with Engineers Without Borders?

The Bhutanese government has recently enacted a Renewable Energy Policy. This is to ensure development in the area, so that the all inhabitants are given access to electricity through sustainable measures. Due to the current national infrastructure, small off-grid PV-systems are a good alternative solution to accommodate this desire and ways for rapid implementation needs to be explored.

This project, working in collaboration with the Royal University of Bhutan is aimed at developing the design tools needed to build a prototype PV-microgrid at the College of Science and Technology in Bhutan. This project will be supporting this Microgrid at CST by proposing a design tool that can identify the following:

- Optimal microgrid structure and composition.
- Full year simulation of the system, with measurements on load, production, voltage and frequency
- Methods for simplifying the planning and resource-assessment phase

The students in this project will be part of a team that is already doing a preliminary assessment that will be used as starting point.
The students might also have the possibility to join a team to do fieldwork on the site of the microgrid implementation in Bhutan.

This project is in collaboration with Engineers Without Borders Norway and the Royal University of Bhutan.


Supervisor: Marta Molinas
Co-supervisors: Håkon Duus (currently involved in first phase of this project)
